STAGE

  

Stages in Sales

Explanation of Stages

Case scenarios

Prospecting

Opportunities in the prospecting stage represent your long term pipeline. No budget or timescale has been identified. This stage is when the closed date is uncertain or either no solid opportunity could be driven out from first conversation to any stage. However, there is a possibility that some of these opportunities will mature into viable and important pipeline deals.

Client wants to think about it and get back/ Client isn’t giving any clear picture on meetings/ Client didn’t respond to any introductory mail / Clients details are just tapped and we need to still get in touch with him

Qualifying

This stage determines if there is a NEED or there  could be a NEED. This stage determines whether the customer sees some interest and is looking forward to talk more on the product or enquire on the same. Also, are we a good fit among potential other suppliers for the client

Customer is using jars / customer is unhappy with the current vendor etc.

Need Analysis

This stage is when we do an analysis on kind of solution client is looking for. This stage can be defined in a nutshell by taking the details over the phone via workbook or going for Site survey

Site survey/ Workbook filling/ seeking the clients site details

Value Proposition

This stage is when the client asks us for cost analysis / proposal submission etc.

 

Evaluating

This stage is when the client is making a decision to come back post submitting the proposal. Product Demo can also fall under Evaluating

 

Negotiating

This stage includes agreeing on commercial terms

 

Closure

The customer made a commitment to go ahead.
